Taj Mahal

The symbol of love, the Taj Mahal, was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan for his wife, Mumtaz Mahal. It's one of the Seven Wonders of the World.

Red Fort

The Red Fort in Delhi, built by Shah Jahan in the 17th century, is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a symbol of India's rich history.

Hawa Mahal

A stunning palace in Jaipur, known as the "Palace of Winds," with intricate latticework and over 900 small windows.

Sanchi Stupa

Sanchi Stupa, one of the oldest stone structures in India, is a significant Buddhist monument built by Emperor Ashoka.

Agra Fort

Agra Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage site in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India. It was built by Mughal emperor Akbar in 1565. The fort was the main residence of the Mughal emperors until 1638.

Ram Mandir

The Ram Mandir in Ayodhya, India is a Hindu temple complex that was inaugurated in January 2024. It's believed to be the birthplace of Rama, a central figure in Hinduism.

Nlanda Mahavihara

The ancient Nalanda University was located in the town of Rajgir in the Bihar state of India. The ruins of the university are still visible today.
